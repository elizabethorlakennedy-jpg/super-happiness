Figure 1 — Annual Project Volume

FDI Projects per Year: NI vs ROI

NI left axis · ROI right axis (÷6 for visual scale) · 2016–2025 · Source: fDi Markets (full datasets)

NI averaged ~35 projects per year, ranging from 26 (2023) to 47 (2016). ROI averaged ~234 per year, peaking at 290 in 2022 before contracting. The relative consistency of project volumes in NI contrasts the cyclical peaks evident in ROI, reflective of NI's smaller total adressable market, less exposed to macreconomic headwinds.

Figure 6 — Project Type

New vs Expansion vs Co-Location

Absolute project counts and % share · NI vs ROI · 2016–2025 · Source: fDi Markets (full datasets)

NI's expansion share (42.8%) is proportionally higher than ROI's (35.8%), suggesting strong investor retention in NI's concentrated tech base. However in absolute terms ROI recorded 837 expansion projects against NI's 151 — 5.5× more. The higher NI share reflects a concentrated base of repeat investors, not superior expansion performance in aggregate and may also indicate a relative underperformance in NI in attracting first-time investors at scale.

Section 7 — Strategic Conclusions

Four Strategic Priorities for NI's FDI Pipeline

Derived from sectoral, activity-type and benchmark analysis · Forward-looking to 2030

01
Deepen and Extend the Technology Cluster
NI's concentration on Software & IT Services (43.3% of all FDI projects) is the defining characteristic of its investment profile. This level of specialisation, nearly 1.5x ROI's own concentration in the same sector, relfects compounding cluster effects. NI should pursue deepening or related diversification.
02
Capitalise on the R&D Advantage
NI's R&D activity share (26.1%) is nearly double ROI's (13.8%) — the most differentiated feature of NI's FDI portfolio. Active promotion to innovation-led investors, in partnership with QUB, Ulster University, and Catapult centres, should be a central strategic priority.
03
Protect & Grow the Expansion Base
The 42.8% expansion rate is NI's most strategically significant FDI metric. Sustained investment in aftercare programmes, talent pipeline development, and investor relations is critical — particularly as global headcount pressures intensify among US tech multinationals.
04
Address the Headquarters Gap
NI attracts only 4.2% of projects as HQ activity vs ROI's 14.0%. While Dublin's EU advantages are a structural post-Brexit constraint, NI could target regional or divisional headquarters — particularly from US investors already operating in the jurisdiction.
